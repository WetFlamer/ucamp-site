Interesting life, usually on the road

For thousands of years, people have built walls to keep themselves away from wild animals;

Today, people are beginning to step out of the walls and come into contact with nature, which can be said to be an exploration and feeling of their own primitive wildness.

In 1906, British camping enthusiast T.H. Holding, based on his experience of camping in the Mississippi River, launched the "Camper's Guide", which described the benefits of camping: "It taught people many ways of self-reliance, increased people's physical activity, and thus often extended their lives, aroused people's interest in life and love for nature.". Once the book was published, it aroused worldwide interest in camping.

In order to help teenagers build a strong body, the United States established the BSA (American Boy Scout) in 1910. Its activities include camping outdoors. BSA is the largest youth organization in the United States, which has greatly promoted the promotion of camping culture.

"Camping is not only a way of travel, but also an aesthetic of life."

When we live in cities, it is easy to forget the existence of nature, and a camping trip can bring people closer to nature and calm their hearts. Feeling the fireworks in nature, chatting under the sky, and watching an outdoor movie by the campfire are all excellent ways to relax.


In the past, people set up tents to huddle together for warmth and seek to be born in the wilderness. Today's people choose to camp in order to integrate into nature and release their free nature.

A clear sky, a piece of grass, a tent, a pot of coffee, and three or five friends can build a utopia of their own, even if they are trapped in the city.

Or go to the edge of the city, find a piece of grass in the mountains, a forest by the lake, or a beach by the sea, and enjoy the wonderful feeling of walking between modern convenience and natural wildness.

"Just hide in a tent, and you can stay away from all the troubles of work." Go back to nature and camp, and feel the peace of the moment with your heart ".

Compared with the statement that "camping is a temporary escape from the city", we prefer to regard camping as a private way to recharge our body and mind. "When we go back to the city after spending a night or two outside, I feel like I'm filling up with energy again."
